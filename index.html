What is the dividend yield?
The dividend yield is a financial ratio that measures the amount of cash dividends that is paid to the shareholders in relation to the market value per share.
How to calculate dividend yield?
To calculate dividend yield you should use the following formula:
dividend yield = (annual dividends per share / current share price) * 100
Example: company x's annual dividend is $2.3 and the stock currently trades at $38, calculate the dividend yield.
Using the dividend yield formula the answer will be:
dividend yield = (annual dividends per share / current share price) * 100
dividend yield = ($2.3 / $38) * 100
dividend yield = 6%
Why use dividend yield?
The dividend yield is used because it shows how much a copany has paid in dividends in a full year and the dividend yield is represented as a percentage.
